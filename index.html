About Us

We are a chairty-based organization who aims to make a difference through various fundraising and awareness campaigns.

Movember Canada

Movember Canada is an organization centered around four pillars: prostate cancer, mental health, testicular cancer, and physical inactivity. Its vision is to have an everlasting impact on the face of men's health. The charity has raised $677 million and funded over 1000 programs. The Prefects are running Movember, a month long event in November raising awareness of the aforementioned pillars. Students and staff can show their support by growing mustaches, donating online, and getting the people in their lives involved.

Poverty

Affecting millions of people worldwide, poverty is an enemy that comes in many shapes and forms. Though it is prevalent in many developing third world countries, poverty impacts people in our local community as well. Because of the detrimental impacts poverty can leave, it is important to work towards changing this growing statistic into a shrinking issue.

Mental Health

Mental illness covers a wide range of disorders that affect your mood, thinking and behavior such as depression, schizophrenia, eating disorvers and many more. According to the Canadian Mental Health Association, 1 in 5 Canadians will personally experience a mental illness within their lifetime. The Prefects are working towards getting rid of the stigma that follows the words "mental illness" by educating students and staff on the effects of these disorders and the ways one can keep up their own mental health.

Civil Rights

Civil rights are an important issue because it supports the rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality from intrusions from other individuals, organizations, and governments. They exist to end discrimination amongst people on the basis of race, gender, religion, national origin, age, and sexual orientation. Civil rights are in place to protect individuals, whether they are part of the majority or minority. It supports that everyone has fundamental rights including: life, liberty, speech, press, assembly, and security of persons.

Environment and Wildlife

We believe that it is crucial for us as youth, to protect the planet as a place for all living things to enjoy. That's why this year, Prefects will be supporting Environment and Wildlife as a cause through the World Land Trust's "Buy an Acre" campaign.

Women's Rights

Feminism: advocating social, political, and all other rights of women equal to those of men. This year, Prefects will be supporting women’s rights for those in our community and those all over the world. Whether it be funding educational equality or raising awareness about societal misogyny, we will be doing our best this year to make equal rights for all genders a reality.

Our 2015-2016 Year

In a snapshot

  • Movember

    November 2015

    Movember is an awareness campaign that runs throughout the whole month of November. Movember Canada focuses on four pillars: prostate cancer, testicular cancer, mental health, and physical inactivity. Two stage events will be held consisting of an information booth, where students can learn more about the four pillars, and a photo booth, where students can take pictures with props provided to further the campaign. Informational posts will be delivered using social media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram), and school-wide announcements will be heard every week.

  • Denner's Served: Breakfast Edition

    November 27th, 2015

    Denner’s Served: Breakfast Edition will be this year’s restaurant fundraiser hosted by the Prefects in partnership with Denny’s Courtney Park. It will take place on Friday, November 27th, 2015 from 5:00 to 9:00 p.m. The fundraiser will, once again, feature a sit down dinner for both adults and children of all ages so all family members and friends are welcome to attend! All of the money raised will go to support CARE and their Syrian Refugee Crisis relief efforts to support and assist relive people of all ages who are affected by the crisis.

  • Holiday Drive

    December 2015

    The annual Holiday Drive unifies the entire school community by allowing classes to donate non-perishable items, gift cards, clothes and toys to specified families of need. These families are selected from within the community and provide a list of most-needed items for their classes to use.

  • Brewed Awakening

    December 2015

    Brewed Awakening is a two week long breakfast event where students and staff can purchase a variety of healthy breakfast items from the Prefects Office to kickstart their day. This year we offered breakfast sandwiches, smoothies, tea and various baked goods. All proceeds went towards the Brain and Behavior Research Foundation which supports various scientists researching cures for mental illnesses.

  • Wok'n'Roll

    March 2016

    Wok n Roll is a chance for the school community to support a good cause through buying a delicious lunch of locally and student made Chinese food. Some past menu items have included; bubble tea, fortune cookies, fried rice and chow mein!

  • WoodsTalk

    March 2016

    WoodsTalk is a one-day inschool event where a variety of inspirational speakers have the chance to voice their story to students. This year the theme of our conference will be Mental Health and all proceeds will go towards Huntington's Disease Society of America.

  • Clubbin'

    April 2016

    Clubbin' is an annual school-wide dodgeball tournament that raises school spirit and club funds. The competition is usually held over two days in the gymnasium; the first day being only round robin games and the second day being both round robin and elimination games. Every year the winning team chooses which club gets a $200 cheque from The Prefects.

  • Thrifted

    May 2016

    Thrifted is an annual clothing boutique staged in the school where various hand-made clothes are sold to the student body. Various pieces of clothing such as cuffed and distressed shorts, studded bows and muscle tanks are modeled so students can become familiar with the products and are then sold on a first come first serve bases. This year, Thrifted will be held in May and will feature all the classics such as our shorts and bows as well as much more.

  • SUPER CHARITY WEEK

    May 2016

    Super Charity Week (SCW) is a week packed with a various events happening each day. Events include Hair Extravaganza, Buskerfest, and Teacher Silent Auction. On the last day we always host our huge event which was previous Race To Educate. Stay tuned to find out more details about Super Charity Week 2016!

Terry Fox BQQ

The Terry Fox BBQ is an annual event hosted alongside the school-wide Terry Fox run/walk run by WAA. This is the first Prefects event of the year and brings the entire community out! For $3.00, students can purchase a halal, veggie, or regular beef burger and for $1.00 they can purchase pop ($3.75 for a combo). In addition, a toonie drive is organized for the morning and change is collected from around the school. All proceeds are donated to the Terry Fox foundation and support groundbreaking cancer research.

Thrifted

Thrifted is an annual clothing boutique where various hand-made clothes are sold to the student body. Material from clothes bought from thrift shops or donated by the student body is transformed into t-shirts, shorts, headbands, scarves and bows. The most popular items are the cuffed and distressed shorts made from pants as well as the various styles of bows made from leftover material. Studs are also used as accessories on clothes as well as sold for students to use for their own DIY projects. This event has been running for the past 3 years and over $1000 was collected last year and donated as Nepal disaster relief funds.

Denner's Served

Organized by Stargazers

Denner’s Served was the restaurant fundraiser hosted by the Prefects, in partnership with Denny’s Courtney Park. The fundraiser featured a sit down dinner for both adults and children of all ages. The event took place on March 6th, 2015 and had over 180 attendees. The Prefects raised well over $1800 from this fundraiser! All of the money raised went to support The First Nations Child and Family Caring Society of Canada (FNCFCS), the only national organization serving Aboriginal children and families. In specific, Prefects raised awareness for a cause called Shannen’s Dream. Shannen’s Dream is a campaign run by the FNCFCS that engages both Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal peoples to better understand education inequalities and to take action to ensure all children and young people are able to receive the same education at well built and maintained school facilities that will empower youth to achieve their dreams and for each child to be proud of their distinct cultures and languages.

Movemeber Shave-Off

Organized by J Squared.

In November 2014, The Prefects held their annual Movember campaign, Spread the Stache. Over 50 students participated in the campaign by collecting pledges while several others, both students and teachers, took the initiative to grow a month-long mustache or beard for the final Shave-Off. Overall, we fundraised over $1000 for Movemeber Canada.

Race To Educate

As the finale of Super Charity Week 2014, Race to Educate was an event that engaged the school community with an "Amazing Race-like" activity where students formed teams and participated in challenges. All of the activities in Race to Educate were centred around informing and raising awareness on the issue of education, one of the pillars of Free The Children. Race to Educate raised approximately $4000 to contribute to the overall donation made by The Woodlands School, succeeding in our end goal of building a school in Africa.
